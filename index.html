REINFORCED THERMOPLASTIC PIPES (RTP)

Reinforced thermoplastic pipes (RTP) are pipelines or flow lines covered by high-strength materials such as aramid and other polymers such as high-density polyethylene (HDPE) with cathodic protection. The strength of the pipe is its reinforcement because it behaves like a composite material.

Hydrocarbons

High density polyethylene (HDPE) has higher chemical and physical resistance compared to carbon steel (CS).

Reinforced thermoplastic pipes (RTP) / Flexible composite pipes (FCP) are the real solution to corrosion problems, since in terms of CAPEX and OPEX, polymer-based flexible pipes are superior to carbon steel (CS).

Examples of internal corrosion inside carbon steel (CS) pipelines, where it is possible to observe the comparative disadvantage of making pipelines using this material, compared to the new generation of materials used for the transport of corrosive fluids.

Mining

Mining activities generate both acid drainage and water table rises. This situation occurs in tunnels and in open-pit. Normally, the presence of sulfides and iron oxides generate low PH values which, together with the abrasion of the transported particles, decrease the life of carbon steel.

Example of water table rise in a mine open-pit, where pumps are required to lower the water level.

Use of high-density polyethylene (HDPE) to transport mine water and allow the extraction of minerals in deeper areas.

FOCO (Flexible Oil Composites)

Our company offers its expertise to increase the economic viability of projects such as enhanced oil recovery (EOR) or CO2 capture and storage (CCS), since transporting highly corrosive molecules (H2S & CO2) through carbon steel pipelines, the economic advantages of such projects can be lost.

Water

Depending on the percentage of BSW, different levels of corrosion can occur, which can only be contained by using polymers such as high density polyethylene (HDPE), since its high chemical resistance added to the creation of composite materials, allows to leave aside carbon steel pipes (CS), due to their limited life time.

Example of the installation of an RTP below ground in a shorter time compared to carbon steel (CS).

Internal corrosion caused by the transport of water in the flowlines /line pipes is a problem that companies often have for using carbon steel, causing the loss of economic viability of many projects and production activities

Fittings

Lined fittings are used to internally protect unusually shaped pipe connections. This service helps to improve overall performance because if only straight sections are lined, corroded sections can slowly be connected.

Inspection

Inspection of both the carbon steel (CS) and the internal HDPE liner help to avoid future problems, because if corroded spots with smart piggings can be identified, better decisions can be made after cleaning.

Pipeline Inspection

Pipeline inspection is a part of the pipeline integrity management for keeping the pipeline in good condition. The rules governing inspection are the pipeline safety regulations. In most cases the pipeline is inspected regularly.

Non-metallic
Reinforcements

In order to avoid metal welds, non-metallic reinforcements (polymers) are used in the external area of the pipeline, allowing a better rehabilitation process.
